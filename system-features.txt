Liquor POS System - Extracted System Features
Based on the current frontend and backend implementation in this repository.

1. Authentication and Access Control
- User login supports username or email plus password.
- First-run registration is restricted to creation of the initial admin account only.
- Public self-registration is disabled after the first admin exists.
- JWT-based authenticated sessions are used for protected routes.
- Users can log out and change their passwords.
- Role-based access control is enforced for admin, manager, and cashier users.
- Cashiers have a restricted operational view, while admins and managers can manage catalog, inventory, suppliers, expenses, and reports.
- Admins can create staff accounts for managers and cashiers.
- Admins can update staff roles and remove staff accounts.
- Staff creation generates temporary passwords and can send invite emails when email is configured.

2. Dashboard and Operational Snapshot
- Dashboard shows daily, weekly, and monthly revenue and transaction counts.
- Dashboard shows inventory totals and low-stock alert counts.
- Dashboard includes a 7-day sales trend chart.
- Dashboard includes sales-by-category visualization.
- Cashier dashboard data is scoped to the logged-in cashier's own sales activity.

3. Point of Sale (POS)
- Product search is available during checkout.
- Cart-based checkout supports adding, removing, and changing item quantities.
- Checkout prevents selling more than available stock.
- Checkout supports walk-in customers and linked customer accounts.
- Payment methods supported in the system are cash, M-Pesa, bank, and split.
- Cash checkout records amount paid and calculates change due.
- Sales create invoice numbers automatically.
- Receipts can be previewed and printed after checkout.
- Receipts include business branding, invoice number, customer, payment method, sale type, totals, and footer text.

4. Pricing and Sales Logic
- Each product can have multiple size variants.
- Each variant stores buying price, retail price, wholesale price, and wholesale threshold.
- The system supports retail and wholesale pricing.
- POS can apply wholesale pricing manually from the price list selector.
- POS also auto-applies wholesale pricing when quantity reaches the variant threshold.
- Wholesale customer accounts can unlock wholesale pricing at checkout.
- Sales store sale type, payment method, subtotal, total amount, amount paid, and change due.
- Sale line items store unit price, buying price at time of sale, wholesale-applied flag, and profit margin.

5. Product Catalog Management
- Product records include name, brand, category, description, barcode, image URL, and active status.
- Variants include size, size in ml, pricing, current stock, barcode, and low-stock threshold.
- Products can be created with an initial variant.
- Additional variants can be added to existing products.
- Products and variants can be edited.
- Admins can delete products or individual variants.
- Product browsing supports search by name, variant, category, and brand.
- Product browsing supports stock-status filtering for in stock, low stock, and out of stock.
- Margin visibility is available to management roles, showing retail and wholesale margin percentages.
- Product view includes preferred and cheapest supplier indicators when supplier links exist.
- Product-level supplier price comparison is available for a variant.
- Price comparison includes supplier cost, lead time, preferred supplier flags, and recent price movement history.

6. Inventory Management
- Live stock levels are tracked per variant.
- Inventory pages show current stock, low-stock status, and last restock details.
- Effective low-stock levels can be calculated using system settings.
- High-value items can use a stricter low-stock threshold.
- Reorder suggestions are generated for items at or below their effective low-stock level.
- Reorder suggestions can include preferred supplier, last purchase details, unit cost, minimum order quantity, and lead time.
- Managers and admins can create stock adjustments.
- Stock adjustments support stock-in and stock-out.
- Adjustment reasons include restocking, breakage, theft, promotion, expired, damaged, found, return, and other.
- Adjustment history is recorded in stock movement logs.
- Negative stock is prevented during adjustments and sales.
- Reorder suggestions can create one-tap draft purchase orders when a preferred supplier is linked.

7. Suppliers and Procurement
- Suppliers can be created, viewed, edited, and marked active or inactive.
- Supplier records include contact name, phone, email, address, account number, notes, payment terms, and credit limit.
- Supplier list includes linked SKU counts, purchase volume, outstanding balances, and overdue exposure.
- Supplier-product links can be created per variant.
- Supplier-product links store unit cost, minimum order quantity, lead time, and preferred supplier flag.
- Supplier detail screens show linked SKUs, purchase history, payment history, and supplier profile data.
- Supplier price history is tracked for linked products.
- Preferred supplier links can roll over to the next best option if a preferred link is removed.

8. Purchase Orders, Receiving, and Payables
- Draft purchase orders can be created for suppliers.
- Purchase orders can also be created directly as received stock entries.
- Purchase order statuses include draft, ordered, partially received, and received.
- Goods received operations update stock levels.
- Goods received operations create stock adjustment records tied to the purchase order.
- Receiving can print a GRN (Goods Received Note).
- Purchase order records store invoice reference, notes, totals, amount paid, balance outstanding, and due date.
- Payment status is tracked as unpaid, partial, or paid.
- Additional receiving can be posted against existing purchase orders.
- Supplier payments can be recorded against received purchase orders.
- A payables dashboard highlights total owed, overdue balances, due-this-week balances, and suppliers with open liabilities.
- Open purchase orders and recent receiving history are available in the suppliers workspace.

9. Sales History and Invoice Management
- Sales history can be filtered by period.
- Sales can be searched by invoice number.
- Cashiers only see their own transaction history.
- Managers and admins can view wider sales history.
- Sale detail views show cashier, customer, payment method, sale type, item lines, and total.
- Sales invoices can be opened and printed/downloaded from the sales history area.

10. Customer Management
- Customer profiles can be created and browsed.
- Customer records include name, phone, email, address, notes, and customer type.
- Customer type supports retail and wholesale.
- Customer search supports matching by name and phone.
- Customer records show purchase counts.
- Customer purchase history can be opened from the customer directory.
- Customer purchase history can be searched by invoice number, payment method, or sold item.
- Customer purchase history can be exported as a printable customer report.
- Wholesale customer accounts are used to unlock wholesale checkout flows.

11. Expense Tracking
- Expenses can be recorded by admins and managers.
- Expense records store category, description, amount, expense date, payment method, reference number, and receipt image field.
- Expense categories include rent, salaries, transport, restocking, utilities, maintenance, and other.
- Expenses are listed in a ledger view.
- Expense reporting includes total spend, category breakdown, monthly trend, and highest individual expenses.
- Expense reporting can be exported as a printable expense report.

12. Reporting and Analytics
- Financial P&L reporting is available.
- P&L report calculates gross revenue, cost of goods sold, gross profit, total operating expenses, and net profit.
- Customer sales reporting shows orders, revenue, items sold, and profit by customer.
- Product sales reporting shows units sold, revenue, transactions, and profit for selected products and variants.
- Supplier statement reporting shows purchase volume, payments, and outstanding balances per supplier.
- Accounts payable aging reporting breaks liabilities into aging buckets.
- Purchase history reporting is available by SKU.
- Margin erosion reporting identifies items with low retail or wholesale margins and recent cost increases.
- Top suppliers reporting ranks suppliers by purchase volume and outstanding balances.
- Reporting supports date scoping and entity filters where applicable.
- Reports can be exported as printable PDF-style documents from the UI.

13. Business Settings and Branding
- Admins can manage business name, business logo, and receipt/report footer.
- Uploaded logo is applied across receipts, reports, and layout branding.
- Admins can configure default low-stock threshold.
- Admins can configure high-value item price threshold.
- Admins can configure high-value low-stock threshold.
- Admins can configure minimum margin alert threshold for reports.
- Public settings endpoint exposes branding and operational thresholds needed by the frontend.

14. Account and Profile Features
- Each logged-in user can view their username, email, and role.
- Each logged-in user can change their own password from the account screen.

15. Data Integrity and System Safeguards
- Product, variant, user, invoice, and supplier uniqueness rules are enforced in the data model.
- Stock is decremented on sale and incremented on goods receipt.
- Checkout validates stock before completing a sale.
- Inventory adjustments reject invalid or negative outcomes.
- The sales flow attempts MongoDB transactions and falls back safely when transactions are unavailable.
- Duplicate-entry and validation errors are handled centrally.

16. Security and Platform Features
- Security middleware includes Helmet and API rate limiting.
- CORS is restricted to approved frontend origins, with optional Vercel preview support.
- Request logging is implemented with Morgan in development and Winston-based logging in the app.
- Health check endpoint is available at /api/health.
- Backend serves the built frontend in production deployments.

17. Main Functional Areas Present in the Application
- Authentication and user management
- Dashboard and analytics snapshot
- POS and checkout
- Product and variant catalog
- Inventory and stock adjustments
- Suppliers and supplier-SKU linking
- Purchase orders, receiving, and payables
- Customer management and customer history
- Sales history and invoice printing
- Expense tracking
- Advanced business reports
- Admin settings and branding
